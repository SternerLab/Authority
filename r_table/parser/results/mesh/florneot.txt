A few species of Disterigma are widespread; many are narrow endemics or are at least altitudinally confined to the cold and humid environments of the cloud forests and pramos. The shrubs of Disterigma are typically small and have diverse growth forms that include erect, scandent, prostrate, or cushion-like shrubs. As the number of described species within Disterigma increased over time, the morphological characters that distinguished it from other inferior-ovaried Ericaceae became less clear to the point to raise doubts about its monophyletic status. Recent comprehensive phylogenetic analyses incorporating both morphological and molecular data allowed the identification of a Disterigma clade, where the type species of the genus is found. The Disterigma clade is the subject of this monograph and 32 species are here recognized within it. Disterigma can be distinguished from other Vaccinieae by the presence of a pair of bracteoles inserted right at the apex of the pedicel, which are differentiated in size from other bracts and bracteoles accompanying the flower; another putative synapomorphy of Disterigma is the presence of white embryos. The majority of the species of Disterigma are found in the northern Andes with 26 species growing in the mountains of Colombia and Ecuador; seven of these species are exclusive to this region. Colombia has the greatest number of species followed by Ecuador and Peru.
